Stockholm's public transport system is operated by Storstockholms Lokaltrafik (SL) and serves the entire Stockholm metropolitan region with an integrated network of metro, commuter rail, light rail, buses, and ferries. The metro, known as Tunnelbanan, opened in 1950 and consists of three colour-coded lines (red, green, blue) totalling 108 kilometres across 100 stations. Many of the metro stations on the blue line are famous for their extensive public art installations, with bedrock walls left exposed and painted, leading some commentators to call the system "the world's longest art gallery". The pendeltåg commuter rail network extends the system out to surrounding municipalities including Södertälje and Märsta. Single-zone tickets and 24-hour passes are sold via the SL app, top-up cards, or contactless bank cards at fare gates; cash has not been accepted on board buses since 2007. Punctuality on the metro typically exceeds 95 percent on weekdays. The system carries roughly 800,000 passengers daily.
